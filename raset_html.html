OUR SUBSYSTEMS

AVIONICS & RECOVERY


Avionics encompasses the electronic systems used for communication, navigation, monitoring, and control in aircraft, satellites, and spacecraft. Recovery, on the other hand, refers to the processes and technologies used to safely return spacecraft or aircraft to Earth. This involves designing re-entry vehicles to withstand intense heat, using parachutes to slow descent, and employing landing systems like gear or retro-thrusters for controlled landings.

PAYLOAD


The payload of an aircraft, spacecraft, or satellite refers to the cargo or instruments it carries, which are intended to accomplish its primary mission. In satellites, this often includes scientific instruments, communication transponders, or imaging equipment.The payload is a critical factor in mission planning and design, influencing the vehicle's size, structure, and fuel requirements. Maximizing payload efficiency is essential for achieving mission objectives while maintaining safety and operational effectiveness.

AERODYNAMICS & STRUCTURES


Aerodynamics and structures are fundamental aspects of aerospace engineering, crucial for the design and performance of aircraft and spacecraft. Aerodynamics focuses on the behavior of air as it flows around a vehicle, influencing factors like lift, drag, and stability. Structures, on the other hand, pertain to the physical framework of the vehicle, including its materials and construction methods. This involves ensuring the vehicle can withstand various stresses and strains during operation, such as those experienced during takeoff, flight, and landing.

PROPULSION


Propulsion is the mechanism that drives aircraft, rockets, and spacecraft by generating thrust. In aircraft, propulsion is typically provided by jet engines or propellers, which convert fuel into forward motion by expelling exhaust gases at high speeds. Spacecraft propulsion systems can vary from traditional chemical rockets to advanced technologies like ion thrusters, which use electric fields to accelerate ions and produce thrust. Effective propulsion systems are crucial for achieving and maintaining flight.
